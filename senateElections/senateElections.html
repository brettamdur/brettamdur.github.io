It's hard to deny that the partisan divide between the two major parties in American politics is growing. But how does that play out in our election results? Does increased partisanship mean that red states are getting redder and blue states are getting bluer? And wouldn't that cause elections in each state to become less competitive?

To help find some answers, we looked at election data for all US Senate elections since 1976. The charts on this page show some results of our effort.
